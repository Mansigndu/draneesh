Symptoms & Indications

Previous Cesarean

A prior surgery may influence the safest delivery approach.

Baby Position

Breech or complex fetal positioning can require cesarean delivery.

Maternal Complications

High blood pressure or bleeding concerns may call for surgical delivery.

Labor Complications

Slow progress or fetal distress may make cesarean delivery the safest choice.

Causes & Risk Factors

Why a Cesarean May Be Recommended

  • Placental complications or fetal distress
  • High-risk pregnancy or labor arrest
  • Maternal conditions such as severe hypertension
  • Prior uterine surgery or complicated obstetric history
Diagnosis

Careful Pre-Operative Planning

  • Clinical evaluation of fetal and maternal status
  • Ultrasound and monitoring if needed
  • Blood tests and anesthesia review
  • Discussion of risks, alternatives, and recovery
