Understanding Uterine Fibroids

Uterine fibroids, also known as leiomyomas or myomas, are benign (non-cancerous) tumors that develop in the uterus. These common gynecological conditions affect approximately 40-50% of women during their reproductive years. Fibroids vary in size, number, and location, and can significantly impact menstrual patterns, fertility, and quality of life.

Our comprehensive approach focuses on accurate diagnosis, individualized treatment planning, symptom management, fertility preservation when desired, and utilizing advanced minimally invasive and laparoscopic techniques whenever possible. We prioritize restoring your health while maintaining reproductive function and quality of life through evidence-based medical and surgical interventions.

Common Symptoms

  • Heavy or prolonged menstrual bleeding
  • Pelvic pain, pressure, or discomfort
  • Pelvic pressure or fullness sensation
  • Frequent urination or difficulty emptying bladder
  • Constipation or difficulty with bowel movements
  • Pain during sexual intercourse (dyspareunia)
  • Enlarged or distended abdomen
  • Infertility or recurrent miscarriage

Key Signs & Indicators

Abnormal Bleeding Patterns

Heavy, prolonged, or frequent menstrual bleeding leading to anemia, fatigue, and significant disruption to daily activities and quality of life.

Pelvic Symptoms

Chronic pelvic pain, pressure, heaviness, or fullness sensation caused by fibroid location and size affecting surrounding pelvic structures.

Urinary & Bowel Impact

Frequent urination, urinary retention, constipation, or bowel dysfunction resulting from fibroid compression of adjacent organs.

Reproductive Concerns

Infertility, recurrent miscarriage, or compromised pregnancy outcomes depending on fibroid location and distortion of the uterine cavity.

Causes & Risk Factors

Understanding Fibroid Development

  • Age (more common in reproductive and perimenopausal years)
  • Genetic predisposition and family history
  • Estrogen and progesterone receptor sensitivity
  • Uterine injury or inflammation
  • Obesity and metabolic factors
  • Ethnicity (higher prevalence in African-descended women)
  • Growth factor and cytokine abnormalities
Diagnostic Evaluation

Comprehensive Fibroid Assessment

  • Detailed menstrual, symptom, and medical history
  • Thorough clinical pelvic examination
  • Pelvic ultrasound imaging
  • Transvaginal ultrasound for detailed assessment
  • MRI evaluation when indicated
  • Complete blood count and iron studies
  • Hysteroscopy for cavity assessment when needed

Personalized Treatment Options

Medical & Hormonal Management

Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs, hormonal contraceptives, progestin therapy, and GnRH agonists to reduce bleeding, manage pain, and slow fibroid growth while preserving fertility.

Minimally Invasive Procedures

Uterine artery embolization, MRI-guided focused ultrasound, and hysteroscopic myomectomy for fibroid reduction with faster recovery and minimal tissue trauma.

Advanced Laparoscopic Surgery

State-of-the-art laparoscopic myomectomy with precise fibroid removal, uterine preservation, and fertility restoration using advanced surgical techniques and instruments.

Frequently Asked Questions

Uterine fibroids are benign (non-cancerous) tumors that develop in or around the uterus. They are extremely common, affecting approximately 40-50% of women during their reproductive years, with higher prevalence in women of African descent. Fibroids can vary greatly in size, number, and location within the uterus. Most fibroids develop without any obvious cause, though genetic predisposition, age, and hormonal factors appear to play important roles in their development and growth.

No, not all fibroids require surgery. Many small, asymptomatic fibroids can be monitored safely with regular ultrasound imaging without intervention. Treatment recommendations depend on fibroid size, location, symptoms, and your reproductive goals. Symptomatic fibroids may be managed with medical therapy including NSAIDs, hormonal contraceptives, or progestin therapy. Surgical intervention is considered when symptoms significantly impact quality of life, fertility is compromised, or medical management proves insufficient. Dr. Sabnis will discuss all available options during your consultation.

Fibroids can affect fertility and pregnancy depending on their size and location. Fibroids within the uterine cavity (submucosal) are most likely to impact conception and pregnancy. They may distort the cavity, interfere with embryo implantation, or obstruct the fallopian tubes. However, many women with fibroids conceive naturally without difficulty. Fibroids located outside the uterine cavity typically don't affect fertility significantly. If you have fibroids and are concerned about conception, Dr. Sabnis can assess your specific situation and recommend appropriate interventions to optimize your fertility potential.

Laparoscopic myomectomy is minimally invasive fibroid removal surgery performed through small incisions (usually 2-3 small ports) in the abdomen. A thin camera (laparoscope) and specialized instruments allow the surgeon to visualize and carefully remove fibroids while preserving the uterus. This advanced technique offers significant advantages over traditional open surgery including smaller incisions, reduced pain, faster recovery, shorter hospital stay, and minimal scarring. Dr. Sabnis has extensive expertise in complex laparoscopic fibroid removal, enabling uterine preservation and fertility restoration for eligible candidates.
