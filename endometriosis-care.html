About the Service

Personalized Care for Endometriosis

Endometriosis can cause chronic pelvic pain, painful periods, and fertility challenges. Dr. Aneesh Sabnis offers diagnostic evaluation and treatment options tailored to the severity of symptoms and the patient’s reproductive goals.

Treatment may include medication, minimally invasive surgery, or a combination of approaches depending on the individual case.

Common Symptoms

  • Severe period pain
  • Pelvic pain outside menstruation
  • Heavy or irregular bleeding
  • Difficulty conceiving

Symptoms & Indicators

Pelvic Pain

Chronic or cyclical pain may indicate endometriosis and requires evaluation.

Pain During Intercourse

Pain with intimacy can be a common symptom of the condition.

Fatigue

Long-term pain and inflammation can contribute to ongoing tiredness.

Infertility

Endometriosis can affect fertility and may need specialized treatment.

Causes & Risk Factors

What Can Contribute

  • Retrograde menstruation or tissue implantation
  • Hormonal factors and inflammatory responses
  • Family history and genetic predisposition
  • Delayed diagnosis or untreated symptoms
Diagnosis

How the Condition Is Assessed

  • Detailed history and symptom review
  • Ultrasound and pelvic examination
  • Advanced imaging when required
  • Evaluation for fertility impact and pain severity

Treatment Options

Medical Management

Pain relief and hormonal treatment options can reduce symptoms and inflammation.

Minimally Invasive Surgery

Surgical care may be recommended when pain is persistent or fertility is affected.

Fertility Preservation

Treatment plans can be shaped around future pregnancy goals and quality of life.

Benefits of Treatment
  • Less chronic pain and improved day-to-day comfort
  • Better fertility planning when needed
  • Improved cycle regularity and symptom control
  • Greater reassurance and confidence in care
Treatment Process
  • Consultation and symptom review
  • Diagnostic evaluation and imaging
  • Treatment plan development
  • Ongoing follow-up and adjustments
