About the Service

Comprehensive Diagnosis and Management

PCOS and fibroids are two of the most common gynecological conditions affecting women of reproductive age. They may influence menstruation, fertility, pain, and long-term health, and often benefit from individualized treatment.

The goal is to improve symptoms, protect fertility where possible, and help restore wellbeing through evidence-based medical and surgical care.

Common Symptoms

  • Irregular or heavy periods
  • Pelvic pain or pressure
  • Acne, hair growth, or weight concerns
  • Difficulty with conception

Signs & Indicators

Heavy Bleeding

Excessive menstrual bleeding may occur with fibroids or hormonal disorders.

Pelvic Pressure

A feeling of fullness or pressure can be linked to fibroid growth.

Acne / Hair Growth

These symptoms are common in women with PCOS and insulin-related imbalance.

Infertility

Hormonal changes can affect ovulation and conception.

Causes & Risk Factors

Underlying Contributors

  • Hormonal imbalance and insulin resistance
  • Genetic factors and family history
  • Fibroid growth influenced by estrogen exposure
  • Weight-related and metabolic factors
Diagnosis

Clinical and Imaging Assessment

  • History review and symptom evaluation
  • Pelvic ultrasound and hormone testing
  • Blood work and reproductive assessment
  • Customized treatment recommendations

Treatment Options

Medical Treatment

Hormonal therapy and lifestyle-based management can reduce symptoms and support ovulation.

Minimally Invasive Procedures

Advanced options can help reduce fibroid burden while supporting recovery.

Fertility Preservation

Treatment planning is designed to preserve reproductive goals whenever possible.

Benefits of Treatment
  • Reduced pain and excessive bleeding
  • Improved fertility and cycle regularity
  • Better metabolic and hormonal balance
  • Enhanced quality of life and confidence
Treatment Process
  • Consultation and symptom review
  • Diagnostic testing and imaging
  • Personalized treatment plan
  • Follow-up and long-term management
