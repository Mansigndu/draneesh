About the Service

Comprehensive Diagnosis and Management

PMOS (Polyendocrine Metabolic Ovarian Syndrome), formerly known as PCOS, is a multisystemic hormonal and metabolic condition affecting reproductive, endocrine, and metabolic health. Combined with uterine fibroids, these conditions require integrated, personalized care addressing hormonal balance, fertility, metabolic function, and symptom relief.

Our approach focuses on managing symptoms, protecting reproductive potential, and reducing long-term health risks through evidence-based medical management and minimally invasive interventions tailored to your individual needs.

Common Symptoms

  • Irregular, missed, or heavy menstrual periods
  • Excess hair growth, acne, and skin changes
  • Weight gain or difficulty managing weight
  • Pelvic pain, pressure, or discomfort
  • Difficulty conceiving or infertility
  • Mood changes and fatigue

Signs & Indicators

Menstrual Irregularities

Absent, irregular, or prolonged periods due to hormonal imbalance and abnormal ovulation patterns.

Hyperandrogenism Symptoms

Excess facial and body hair, acne, and male-pattern hair loss linked to elevated androgen levels.

Metabolic Concerns

Insulin resistance, weight gain, difficulty losing weight, and increased risk of type 2 diabetes.

Reproductive Impact

Irregular ovulation, reduced fertility, and increased miscarriage risk require specialized support.

Causes & Risk Factors

Underlying Contributors to PMOS

  • Insulin resistance and impaired glucose metabolism
  • Elevated androgen (male hormone) production
  • Genetic predisposition and family history
  • Low-grade chronic inflammation
  • Obesity and metabolic syndrome
  • Environmental and lifestyle factors
Diagnosis

Comprehensive Diagnostic Evaluation

  • Detailed menstrual and symptom history
  • Hormone and androgen level blood testing
  • Glucose and insulin metabolism assessment
  • Transvaginal pelvic ultrasound imaging
  • Anti-Müllerian hormone (AMH) evaluation
  • Metabolic risk stratification

Treatment Options

Lifestyle & Metabolic Management

Structured diet, exercise, and weight management to improve insulin sensitivity, regulate hormones, and reduce long-term health risks.

Hormonal & Medical Therapy

Oral contraceptives, metformin, anti-androgens, and fertility medications tailored to symptoms and reproductive goals.

Reproductive & Surgical Options

Ovulation induction, laparoscopic procedures, and fibroid management to support conception and symptom relief.

Benefits of Treatment
  • Regular, predictable menstrual cycles
  • Improved insulin sensitivity and metabolic health
  • Reduced androgen-related symptoms (acne, hair growth)
  • Enhanced fertility and increased conception rates
  • Lower long-term risk of diabetes and cardiovascular disease
  • Improved mood, energy, and quality of life

Frequently Asked Questions

PMOS (Polyendocrine Metabolic Ovarian Syndrome) is the updated terminology for what was previously called PCOS. This name change reflects better understanding of the condition as a multisystemic disorder affecting hormones, metabolism, and reproductive function—not just the ovaries. The diagnostic criteria remain the same, but PMOS emphasizes the need for comprehensive metabolic and endocrine management.

There is no cure for PMOS, but symptoms can be significantly managed through lifestyle modifications including structured diet, regular exercise, weight management, and stress reduction. Many patients benefit from medical therapy like metformin, hormonal contraceptives, or anti-androgens depending on their specific symptoms and goals. A combination approach often yields the best results.

PMOS commonly causes irregular ovulation or anovulation, which reduces fertility. However, with appropriate treatment including ovulation induction medications, weight management, and metabolic optimization, many women with PMOS successfully conceive. Early consultation and assessment of ovarian reserve (AMH levels) help guide fertility planning.

Women with PMOS have increased risk of type 2 diabetes, cardiovascular disease, metabolic syndrome, and endometrial cancer due to chronic insulin resistance and hormonal imbalance. Regular screening, metabolic monitoring, and proactive lifestyle management significantly reduce these risks. Dr. Sabnis provides comprehensive risk stratification and preventive care planning.

Most PMOS cases are managed with medical and lifestyle treatment without surgery. However, if fibroids are causing significant symptoms or infertility, minimally invasive options like laparoscopy or hysteroscopy may be recommended. Laparoscopic ovarian drilling is occasionally used for ovulation induction when medications are unsuccessful. Treatment is always personalized to your specific situation.
