About the Service

Minimally Invasive Solutions for Gynecological Conditions

Laparoscopic surgery offers a modern approach to treating many gynecological conditions with smaller incisions, less pain, and a faster return to daily life. Dr. Aneesh Sabnis uses advanced surgical techniques to manage complex cases with precision and care.

The approach is especially useful when a patient needs surgery but wants a shorter recovery period and lower surgical trauma.

Common Indications

  • Fibroids and ovarian cysts
  • Endometriosis and pelvic adhesions
  • Reproductive surgery and diagnostic procedures
  • Persistent pain requiring surgical evaluation

Symptoms & Clinical Signs

Pelvic Pain

Chronic pain may indicate adhesions, cysts, or endometriosis.

Irregular Bleeding

Persistent bleeding can be associated with structural conditions.

Infertility

Reproductive surgery may be recommended based on diagnosis.

Mass / Pressure

Large or symptomatic cysts and fibroids may need surgical management.

Causes & Risk Factors

Common Drivers of Surgical Need

  • Endometriosis and pelvic scarring
  • Fibroids, cysts, or ovarian masses
  • Recurrent pain after conservative treatment
  • Need for diagnostic clarity and removal of pathology
Diagnosis

Evaluation Before Surgery

  • Clinical examination and symptom review
  • Ultrasound and relevant imaging
  • Blood tests and pre-operative assessment
  • Clear discussion of surgical options and risks

Treatment Options

Diagnostic Laparoscopy

Useful when internal pelvic pathology needs to be confirmed or treated.

Therapeutic Surgery

Removal of cysts, fibroids, or adhesions with minimally invasive access.

Recovery Planning

Post-surgical guidance to help patients return to normal activity safely.

Benefits of Treatment
  • Smaller incisions and less scarring
  • Shorter recovery time and less postoperative pain
  • Improved comfort and faster return to routine
  • Better surgical precision for many conditions
Treatment Process
  • Pre-surgical evaluation and planning
  • Minimally invasive surgery under anesthesia
  • Close post-op monitoring and pain management
  • Recovery follow-up and return-to-activity advice
